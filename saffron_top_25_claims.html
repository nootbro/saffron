Proven Metrics

Top 25 Clinical Findings

Rank Metric Key Findings/Notes
1 As effective as Prozac (fluoxetine) for depression
  • 30mg saffron daily was statistically equivalent to 20mg fluoxetine
  • Both groups showed similar improvements in depression scores
  • No significant difference in HAM-D score improvement (F=0.13, p=0.71)
  • Saffron produced fewer side effects than the pharmaceutical
  • 6-week randomized double-blind trial with 40 adults
2 As effective as imipramine (tricyclic antidepressant) for depression
  • Saffron 30mg/day matched the efficacy of imipramine 100mg
  • No significant difference in HAM-D score improvement (p=0.09)
  • Imipramine caused more anticholinergic side effects (dry mouth, sedation)
  • Saffron was better tolerated overall
  • 6-week pilot double-blind randomized trial with 30 outpatients
3 Significantly outperformed placebo for depression
  • After 6 weeks, saffron produced significantly greater improvement in depression scores
  • HAM-D score reduction was statistically significant (F=18.89, p<0.001)
  • No significant difference in side effects versus placebo
  • Double-blind, randomized and placebo-controlled trial
  • 40 adult outpatients with major depressive disorder
4 Non-inferior to fluoxetine for postpartum depression
  • Saffron was non-inferior to fluoxetine for postpartum depression
  • After 6 weeks, 40.6% of saffron group vs 50% of fluoxetine group achieved ≥50% reduction in depression score
  • No significant difference between groups (p=0.61)
  • Saffron produced similar symptom relief without additional adverse events
  • Double-blind, randomized clinical trial with 68 women
5 As effective as sertraline for geriatric depression
  • Saffron (60mg) was as effective as sertraline (100mg) in older patients
  • Depression scores improved significantly over 6 weeks in both groups
  • No difference between saffron and sertraline groups
  • Highlights saffron's potential as an alternative antidepressant in the elderly
  • Double-blind, randomized intervention study with 54 older adults
6 Reduced snacking and promoted modest weight loss
  • Saffron supplementation reduced between-meal snacking
  • Greater decrease in body weight (–1.1kg vs –0.4kg in placebo, p<0.05)
  • Significantly fewer snack cravings reported
  • Attributed to saffron's mood-elevating effect reducing urge to snack
  • 8-week trial with 60 healthy overweight women
7 Significantly improved sleep quality
  • Sleep quality improved significantly with saffron vs placebo (p<0.05)
  • Insomnia symptoms (ISQ scores) improved more in saffron groups
  • Morning mood also improved more with saffron
  • No differences between 14mg vs 28mg doses
  • Even low-dose saffron enhanced sleep without adverse effects
  • 4-week study with 120 adults with poor sleep
8 Improved cognitive performance in Alzheimer's disease
  • Saffron produced significantly better cognitive outcomes than placebo
  • Greater improvement in ADAS-Cog scores than placebo (F=4.12, p=0.04)
  • Global functioning (CDR score) also improved more with saffron (p=0.04)
  • No difference in adverse events vs placebo
  • 16-week, randomized and placebo-controlled trial with 46 Alzheimer's patients
9 As effective as donepezil for Alzheimer's with fewer side effects
  • Saffron was as effective as donepezil in treating cognitive symptoms
  • No significant difference in ADAS-Cog or CDR scores between groups
  • Saffron caused fewer side effects - vomiting occurred more often with donepezil (p<0.05)
  • Comparable efficacy with better tolerability
  • 22-week, multicenter, randomized, double-blind controlled trial with 54 patients
10 Improved SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction in men
  • Significantly improved erectile function and sexual satisfaction vs placebo
  • 60% achieved normal erectile function vs only 7% in placebo group (p=0.005)
  • Intercourse satisfaction and overall IIEF total score also improved more with saffron (p<0.001)
  • Side effect frequency did not differ from placebo
  • 4-week randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial with 36 men
11 Improved SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction in women
  • Significant improvements in overall female sexual function vs placebo
  • Higher total FSFI scores (female sexual function index) (p<0.001)
  • Better arousal (p=0.028), lubrication (p=0.035), and reduced pain (p=0.016)
  • No difference in side effect frequency observed
  • 4-week randomized double-blind placebo-controlled study with 38 women
12 Alleviated opioid withdrawal symptoms
  • Adding saffron significantly alleviated opioid withdrawal symptoms
  • Lower withdrawal syndrome scores than methadone-only group (p<0.001)
  • Saffron co-therapy helped ease withdrawal in patients tapering off opioids
  • No adverse interactions noted
  • Study with 44 patients on methadone maintenance therapy
13 Reduced anxiety and depression in adolescents
  • Self-reported total RCADS internalizing scores decreased by ~33% with saffron vs 17% with placebo (p=0.029)
  • Improvements in separation anxiety (p=0.003), social phobia (p=0.023), and depressive symptoms (p=0.016)
  • Well tolerated in adolescents
  • 8-week randomized, placebo-controlled study with 80 youths aged 12-16
14 Greater improvement in depression symptoms in teens
  • On the RCADS depression subscale, saffron group scores improved significantly more (mean -9.2 points) than placebo (-4.6) (p=0.016)
  • Parents' ratings also trended better with saffron (40% vs 26% symptom reduction)
  • Between-group differences were mostly noted in self-report
  • 8-week study with adolescents with elevated depression scores
15 As effective as fluvoxamine for OCD
  • Saffron was as effective as fluvoxamine in reducing OCD symptoms
  • Both groups showed significant improvement in Y-BOCS scores over 10 weeks
  • No significant time×treatment interaction (p=0.52)
  • Similar mean reductions in OCD severity
  • Adverse event rates were comparable
  • 10-week trial with 50 adults with mild-to-moderate OCD
16 Comparable to methylphenidate (Ritalin) for ADHD in children
  • Saffron's efficacy was comparable to methylphenidate for ADHD symptoms
  • Both parent and teacher ADHD Rating Scale scores improved similarly (p=0.70)
  • Inattention and hyperactivity/impulsivity sub-scores decreased in parallel
  • Side-effect frequency was similar
  • 6-week randomized double-blind pilot study with 54 children
17 Enhanced stimulant therapy in adult ADHD
  • Combination therapy outperformed stimulant alone
  • When saffron was used alongside methylphenidate, ADHD symptom scores improved more
  • Significantly greater reductions in ADHD rating scale scores with the combo
  • Suggests a synergistic benefit for superior control of symptoms
  • Two clinical trials (2021, 2022) with 50 adults with ADHD
18 Improved sleep in ADHD patients
  • Both saffron and stimulants increased total sleep hours
  • Only saffron-treated groups saw significantly faster sleep onset
  • Better nighttime sleep quality than those on stimulant alone
  • Unique advantage for ADHD patients who often have sleep difficulties
  • Findings from trials with adolescents and adults with ADHD
19 Relieved premenstrual syndrome (PMS) symptoms
  • Greater reduction in total Premenstrual Daily Symptom Report scores than placebo (p<0.001)
  • Marked improvements in mood swings, pain, and cravings during luteal phases
  • Demonstrated efficacy for PMS
  • Two-cycle randomized, placebo-controlled trial with 50 women
20 Improved PMS-related depression and mood
  • Hamilton Depression Rating Scale scores in the late luteal phase were significantly lower with saffron than placebo (p<0.001)
  • Far fewer PMS-related depressive symptoms
  • Beneficial effect on emotional and mood disturbances associated with PMS
  • Two-cycle study with 50 women with PMS
21 Effective for premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD)
  • Significantly reduced emotional and physical symptoms compared to placebo
  • Improvements in mood, irritability, and cramps superior to placebo
  • Minimal adverse effects
  • Can safely help manage the debilitating symptoms of PMDD
  • 8-week study with 60 women with diagnosed PMDD
22 Comparable to Alzheimer's drugs in systematic review
  • Clinical evidence indicates saffron's cognitive benefit in Alzheimer's is on par with approved medications
  • Comparable efficacy to donepezil and memantine on cognitive outcomes
  • More favorable side-effect profile
  • 30mg saffron daily achieved similar ADAS-cog improvements to 10mg donepezil
  • Systematic review of multiple trials
23 Meta-analysis confirmed antidepressant efficacy
  • Comprehensive meta-analysis concluded saffron is as effective as standard antidepressants
  • Depression symptom reductions statistically equivalent to SSRIs
  • Significantly outperformed placebo
  • Fewer side effects than conventional drugs
  • Analysis of 8 randomized trials with approximately 550 participants
24 Excellent safety profile across multiple trials
  • Adverse events on saffron were generally mild and not significantly different from placebo
  • Fewer drug-related side effects than standard medications
  • Less sedation and dry mouth than imipramine
  • Less vomiting than donepezil
  • No serious adverse reactions reported even at doses up to 60mg/day
  • Excellent tolerability in humans across multiple studies
25 Comparable to duloxetine for fibromyalgia
  • Similar improvements in pain intensity (VAS scores) and functional impact (FIQ scores) as duloxetine
  • No statistically significant difference between groups
  • Depressive symptoms (HAM-D) also improved in both groups
  • Well-tolerated with fewer side effects (less nausea) than duloxetine
  • 8-week randomized controlled trial with 61 adults with fibromyalgia
